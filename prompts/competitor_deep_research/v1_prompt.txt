# Research Instructions

For EACH competitor listed above, conduct thorough research across these three layers:

## Layer 1: Pricing & Packages

Starting from the pricing URLs provided above:
- List every package/plan that directly competes with our campaign's offerings
- For each package extract: name, monthly price, unit cost (øre/kWh, kr/GB, kr/md depending on product), binding period, cancellation terms, setup fees, and any included extras
- Create a direct comparison with OUR product from the Campaign Information — state clearly whether they are cheaper or more expensive and by how much
- Note hidden costs: transport fees, "tillæg", price escalation clauses, fees for leaving early
- If prices have changed recently, note the old vs new price

## Layer 2: Customer Pain Points & Stories (Trustpilot, Forums, Social Media)

Starting from the Trustpilot URLs provided above:
- Find verified negative reviews and recurring complaints
- Search Danish forums (Reddit r/Denmark, Pair, Facebook groups) for real customer experiences

CRITICAL FORMATTING RULE — every pain point must follow this structure:
- **claim**: A specific, concrete complaint (NOT generic like "bad service")
- **story**: A paraphrased real customer anecdote with specific details (timeframes, outcomes, what went wrong)

Examples of BAD claims (too vague — do NOT produce these):
- "Customers are unhappy with the service"
- "Many complaints about billing"
- "Poor customer support"

Examples of GOOD claims with stories:
- claim: "Impossible to cancel — requires calling 3 separate times"
  story: "A customer tried to cancel online but was told to call. Called twice, held 45 minutes each time, then told there's a 3-month notice period nobody disclosed at signup."
- claim: "Automatic price increases without notice"
  story: "A customer's electricity bill jumped 40% over 6 months. When they called, they were told the 'introductory offer' had expired — a detail buried in paragraph 14 of the contract."

## Layer 3: Critical News & Regulatory Issues (last 12 months)

Search for recent negative press about each competitor:
- Danish keywords to search: "Forbrugerombudsmanden", "prisstigninger", "nedbrud", "kundeflugt", "bindingsperiode", "klage", "tilsyn"
- Look for: regulatory actions, fines, mass price increases, service outages, data breaches, misleading advertising cases
- For each finding: include the date, a headline-style summary, and why it matters for our sales pitch
