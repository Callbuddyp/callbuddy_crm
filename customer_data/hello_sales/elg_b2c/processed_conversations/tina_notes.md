



## Phase identification
Opener requirements: 
1. The user has not yet confirmed who they are 
2. We have not confirmed that the person is the one capable of taking the product desisicion (for example buy confirming to buy) 
3. We have not gained a indicator, that we can begin the discovery phase. 

Discover requirements: 
1. The customer has accepted that it has recieved a call from a salesperson, and has not made any objections to this
2. We have confirmed that the person is the one responsible for the product 
2. Small pain points, or larger pain points, which the customer has not really acknolwegde as worth fixing, or as true pains.

Pitch requirements:
1. Discovered at pain point, which the seller has acknowlegde, is highly relevant. 

Close requirements:
1. The pitch as been delivered, and the customer agrees to the value. 

## Keypoint identification

Key points requirements: 

1. Novel information, that can be used to pitch the product as defined in [PRODUCT], such as current provider, current cost its corresponding service / product, the reason for what they need
2. Personal information, which can be used to create a relation between the product/pitc and the customer
3. Personal information which can be used to create a bound between the seller and the customer.
4. The information is not present  in the current state
5. Information about their current provider, which can be used to create a better pitch by personalizing it to their needs, or to suggest improvements, or matching their service. Examples of this is current price, whether they just changed provider, the size of the package, knowlegde about binding
